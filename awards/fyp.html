Final Year Project provides students with the opportunity to showcase the abilities and talents they have acquired during their time at WKWSCI. Since its inception, one of the goals of the WKWSCI Final Year Project is for students to complete a project that reaches a wider audience than the school. WKWSCI students do campaigns for clients that reach broad audiences. They produce videos that are shown at international festivals and win international competitions. They produce impactful journalism that is published in mainstream media. Our students have a remarkable record of completing research projects that are presented at international conferences and published in international, peer-reviewed academic journals.

The Top FYP Awards are awarded to fourth-year undergraduates who have submitted the most impressive final-year projects under the respective categories - Journalism, Research, Campaigns, Broadcast. *Awarded by the school

Award Recipients

The Stray Moth

Top Final Year Project (Broadcast)

Barnabas Chua Zhong Li
Chua Shu Ern
Huang Jun Yao Kenneth
Tan Pat Hoon Megan

‘The Stray Moth’ is a narrative film that depicts the story of Kian, a man who has kept his gender identity a secret to himself. The film explores Kian’s journey as he comes face to face with his own gender identity through getai.

The Age of Change

Top Final Year Project (Journalism)

Kimberly Ng
Sherlyn Seah Xue Ning
Shirley Tay Yong Yun

‘The Age of Change’ is a journalism feature writing project that explores the ongoing efforts to eliminate child marriages in the Malaysian state of Sarawak, the state with the highest number of such unions in the country. The project aims to highlight the evolving landscape of child rights and marriage reform as the state undergoes a concurrent age of change.

Ready, Steady, Go

Top Final Year Project (Campaign)

Ng Jing Xuan Joshua
Wong Yi Ting Jade
Yeo Sze-G
Ysabelle Torres

‘Ready, Steady, Go’ is a health communication campaign aimed at fall prevention among seniors over 60 years old by promoting strength, balance and flexibility exercises. The campaign utilised mobile technology, physical roadshows, social media outreach and exercise tutorial videos to engage more than 1,000 elderly to subscribe to their WhatsApp Programme, and empowered over 500 older adults to participate in community exercise programme.

News Grazing in the Era of Information Overload

Top Final Year Project (Research)

Hong Li Xuan
Pong Xiang Yuan Brenda
Rachelle Lye Wan Xin
Sng Ngiag Gya Trisha

‘News Grazing in the Era of Information Overload’ is an applied research project that sought to understand the phenomenon of news grazing – the act of skimming through bite-sized news intermittently, or at regular interstices of time, in a summarised format, underpinned by the prioritisation of efficiency. The study identified the motivations for news grazing and technical affordances that facilitate news grazing and found that users are motivated by the efficiency of fulfilling their informational and social needs.
